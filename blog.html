Who is Michael Joseph Jackson?

Michael Joseph Jackson (August 29, 1958 - June 25, 2009) was an American singer, songwriter, and dancer. Dubbed the "King of Pop", he is regarded as one of the most significant cultural figures of the 20th century. Jackson's contributions to music, dance, and fashion made him a global figure in popular culture for over four decades. Alongside his older brothers Jermaine, Tito, Marlon, and Jackie, he made his professional debut in 1964 as a member of the Jackson 5.

He went on to become a solo artist with his studio albums "Off the Wall" (1979), "Thriller" (1982), "Bad" (1987), "Dangerous" (1991), and "HIStory: Past, Present and Future, Book I" (1995). With his innovative rhythms, vocal style, and choreography, Jackson popularized a number of music techniques such as the robot and the moonwalk. His music videos, such as "Thriller", "Beat It", and "Billie Jean", were milestones in the medium and helped bring the concept of the music video to the mainstream.

Jackson's personal life was the subject of extensive media scrutiny, with his changing physical appearance and relationships being a source of tabloid speculation.

Jackson died on June 25, 2009, at the age of 50 from a combination of drugs in his system. He is one of the best-selling music artists of all time, with estimated sales of over 350 million records worldwide. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice, and his awards include 13 Grammy Awards, 6 Brit Awards, and 39 Guinness World Records.
